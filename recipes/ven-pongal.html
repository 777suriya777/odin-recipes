Description

Ven Pongal is a traditional and beloved South Indian breakfast dish made with rice and moong dal (yellow split lentils). It is a savory, comfort food that is simple to prepare yet packed with flavor. The dish is typically tempered with a mixture of black pepper, cumin seeds, ginger, and curry leaves, giving it a fragrant and mildly spicy flavor. Cashews are added for crunch, and ghee (clarified butter) is generously poured over the top to enhance the richness.

Ven Pongal is often served with sambar (a spicy lentil stew) and coconut chutney, making it a wholesome and satisfying meal. It is commonly eaten during festivals, special occasions, or as part of a traditional South Indian breakfast. Its combination of soft rice and dal with aromatic spices makes it both nourishing and delicious.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Moong dal (yellow split lentils)
  • Water
  • Black pepper
  • Cumin seeds
  • Ginger
  • Curry leaves
  • Cashews

Steps

  1. Roasting the Moong Dal
  2. Cooking Rice and Moong Dal
  3. Tempering (Tadka)
  4. Mixing the Tempering into Pongal
  5. Garnish and Serve
