Description

Sambar is a popular South Indian dish that is a flavorful and nutritious lentil-based stew. It is made with cooked lentils (usually toor dal or split pigeon peas), a variety of vegetables like drumsticks, carrots, pumpkins, and okra, and a tangy tamarind base. The dish is seasoned with a unique blend of spices, typically prepared using sambar powder, which contains roasted and ground spices like coriander seeds, cumin, fenugreek, and dried red chilies.

The preparation is completed with a tempering of mustard seeds, curry leaves, dried red chilies, and asafoetida (hing), which is added for an aromatic finish. Sambar is a versatile dish and can be paired with steamed rice, idli, dosa, or vada, making it a staple in South Indian cuisine. Its tangy, spicy, and mildly sweet flavors create a perfect harmony, making it both a comforting everyday meal and a delightful dish for festive occasions.

Ingredients

  • Toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • Tamarind
  • Drumsticks
  • Carrots
  • Brinjal
  • Beans
  • Potatoes
  • Ladies' fingers
  • Tomato
  • Water

Steps

  1. Cook the Dal
  2. Prepare Tamarind Water
  3. Cook the Vegetables
  4. Add Spices and Dal
  5. Tempering
  6. Garnish and Serve
